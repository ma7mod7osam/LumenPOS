## The business

[Retailer name] runs [three] retail branches across [Riyadh], selling [cameras, accessories and general electronics]. They'd been on ERPNext for their inventory and accounting for [two years] and were happy with the back office — stock, purchasing and financials all lived in one place. The counter was the weak spot.

At the till they were using [the stock ERPNext POS / a separate POS that didn't talk to ERPNext], and on a busy afternoon it showed.

## The challenge

A few things kept biting them:

- **Queues at peak.** Each sale meant waiting on the screen to catch up — page reloads, lookups, and a payment flow with too many steps. During weekend rushes, lines built up and staff felt the pressure.
- **Lost sales when the connection dropped.** One branch had unreliable internet. When it went down, the till simply stopped — and so did selling, until the connection came back.
- **Promotions were a project.** Running a "buy 2 get 1" or a weekend bundle meant fighting with pricing settings in the back office, and prices sometimes didn't match what staff expected at the till.
- **The drawer never matched.** Closing the till at night was guesswork. Cash counts drifted from what the system thought they should be, and nobody could say exactly why.
- **No single view across branches.** Because the counter data didn't flow cleanly into ERPNext, head office was stitching numbers together after the fact.

## Why LumenPOS

They wanted the speed of a purpose-built retail till **without** moving off ERPNext or running a second system to reconcile. LumenPOS fit because it isn't a separate silo — every sale posts as a normal ERPNext invoice, with the right stock and ledger entries behind it. The selling experience is just faster and made for a counter.

Three things sealed it:

1. **It keeps working offline** — exactly the problem their unreliable branch had.
2. **Promotions live in the POS**, so staff could set up offers without back-office gymnastics.
3. **Real register sessions** with a counted close, so the drawer would finally balance.

## The rollout

Setup was light. Because LumenPOS ships pre-built, installing it was a standard app install on their existing site — no separate servers, no new database. The team:

- pointed each branch at its **POS Profile** (company, price list, warehouse, payment methods),
- added cashiers and set who could **discount** and **refund**,
- designed the **receipt** (logo, tax ID, Arabic/English) in Settings,
- and let the catalogue **cache for offline use** on each till.

Staff training took [a single short session] — the sell screen is one page, so cashiers picked it up quickly.
